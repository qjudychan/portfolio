  HABIT  

(WORK IN PROGRESS)
The Habit app endeavors to help those in pursuit of self improvement help themselves by bringing behavioral research insights to productivity tools (to-do lists, timers, counters)

  RESEARCH  


Misalignment between intentions and ultimate actions is a common pitfall in trying to develop good habits. Luckily, as an oft-researched subject in behavioral science, there are studies on habit formation from which we can draw insights:


LITERATURE REVIEW
  • How are habits formed: Modelling habit formation in the real world, Lally et al, 2010
  • Forming a Flossing Habit, Judeh et al, 2013
  • Choice and Procrastination, O'Donoghue and Rabin, 2000


These studies informed the user journey below, which features 3 hurdles that an individual faces when attempting to turn a behavior into a habit, each of which also serves as a potential reason why he/she may disengage from the goal:


Set goal→ → Forgetfulness → → Procrastination → → Waning Diligence
Description Individual struggles to recollect delayed intentions Individual's preferences are time inconsistent - "later" is more appealing Desired behavior declines in frequency as time progresses, undermining automaticity
Opportunities Deploy daily reminders to use the productivity tools offered in the app, with the ultimate goal of committing behaviors to memory (effortlessly recalled behaviors are more likely to performed) Encourage daily reflection on one's goals to help sustain motivation after the initial determination to try and form the habit Build motivation by engaging the individual with information about others pursuing similar goals

Reduce perceived effort by making the tracking of habits as easy as possible


In designing to accomodate these insights about forming habits, it becomes apparent that there are two simultaenous goals at hand:

Goal 1. Develop automaticity when it comes to utilizing the app, which in turn ideally helps facilitate Goal 2

Goal 2. Form healthy habits of choice





   IDEATION   


How might we facilitate self evaluation and accountability towards building desirable habits?

Initial sketches were developed into the following wireframes



Daily Survey

View and keep track of progress towards one's goals for the day on the homescreen

For convenience, productivity tools (counter and timer) are all usable on this one screen

Futhermore, users will receive a daily reminder (at night) to go through the survey and record the extent to which they engaged in the intended behaviors


Goal Inspirations

Choose from a list of common habits

This allows for comparisons with other users who have the same habit, which helps build motivation and accountability


Set goal specifics

Customize the goal (e.g., frequency, whether it's a checklist item or timed item)


My history

Analytics are provided to help the user reflect on their progress

If the goal is one of the suggested habits, the user may also benchmark their progress relative to others'
