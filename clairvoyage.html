  RESEARCH  


Six user interviews, which focused on travel accommodation experiences and selection approaches, informed the traits and challenges featured in the following persona and corresponding journey map



INTERVIEW FINDINGS
  • Negative travel accommodation experiences are often tied to:
  • Misconceptions regarding location and proximity (e.g., hotel is farther from activities than one realized)
  • Disillusionment due to insufficient / misleading online portrayals
  • Planning is typically done on desktop due to screen size and complexity of research




MILLENNIAL
MARGO
  • Medium to high technology proficiency
  • Selective - values research and comparing options
  • Future-oriented - derives utility from planning / anticipation

              
GOALS
  • Pre-plan to optimize the trip:
  • Maximize activities/sights
  • Stay at nicest place feasible
  • Visit all the trendy spots on social media (e.g., Instagram, Pinterest)
PERSONA &
USER JOURNEY


Plan Itinerary Plan Accommodation Experience
Steps 1. Browsing travel-related content (e.g., Instagram, Pinterest) 2. Locale-specific research (e.g., Tripadvisor, LonelyPlanet) 3. Selecting a hotel location (geographical research) 4. Filtering based on preferences (e.g., price, amenities, etc.) 5. Reading reviews to confirm or dismiss potential selections 6. Realizing the infeasibility of certain aspects of the itinerary
Happiness

Painpoints Premature expectations may be formed in absence of sufficient consideration of logistics, which often must be researched separately Toggling between multiple sources (if one even remembers to) can be overwhelming

Uncertainty over the weight to assign to a stranger's opinions 		Canceling anticipated activities due to planning oversights is disappointing
Opportunities Incorporate consideration of logistics earlier in the planning process Streamline research by: (i) presenting customized information based on user's interests and (ii) aggregating different types of information under one tool

Focus on objective considerations (e.g., photos from visitors) that allow user to draw own conclusions		 Providing a visual representation of the plan facilitates identifying potential inconveniences

   IDEATION   


How might we customize the accomodation selection process to allow for more informed decision making?

Sketches → Wireframes


Search / Discover

Specify a destination or take inspiration and browse a trending locale (in which case specifics (e.g., travel dates) can be defined later on)

Larger, more legible text in lieu of thumbnails fits more destination options without the need for scrolling


Itinerary Catalog

Build an itinerary by selecting from presented popular attractions at the chosen destination

Option to add itinerary items by address allows for customization for more esoteric interests


Itinerary Cart

Rank items in one's itinerary cart based on importance

Preferences as indicated in this step factor into how information is presented about potential hotels


Custom Map

See itinerary cart items and potential hotels on one map

Hotels are presented based on proximity to items in the user’s itinerary cart but can be narrowed down through filtration


Map Filtration

Narrow down potential hotels by applying one's preferences

Filtration is "live" - the number of hotel options updates every time the user edits criteria

After selection of preferences, users can “uncheck” one of the filters to easily see how options change if they compromise on a certain criteria


Hotel Detail

Browse a summary of a selected hotel, which covers proximity to itinerary item, which desired amenities are met, etc.

How far a hotel is from itinerary items is prominently presented in order to mitigate overlooking this consideration, which normally would have to be researched separately



  PROTOTYPE  


Phase 1. Itinerary

Margo selects Beijing from the list of trending destinations on the homescreen

A catalog of popular attractions in Beijing is presented

Margo browses the short descriptions and visitors' photos that are available, adding items of interest to her itinerary list

Upon settling on a list of places that she wants to see, Margo ranks her itinerary items based on priority


Phase 2. Hotels

A custom map displaying her itinerary items alongside potential nearby hotels allows Margo to visualize the geographical breadth of her plan

Filters (e.g., trip dates, budget) can easily be toggled on and off to compare options if she compromises on certain considerations

Ensuring a focus on logistics, when Margo selects a hotel on the map, she sees distance from her itinerary items (top three based on rank) prominently presented
